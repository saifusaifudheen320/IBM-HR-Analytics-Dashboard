# IBM-HR-Analytics-Dashboard
HR Attrition Analytics Dashboard built with Power BI, SQL and Excel

## Project Overview
An end-to-end HR Analytics Dashboard analyzing employee attrition 
across 1,470 employees using the IBM HR Dataset.

## Tools Used
- Power BI — Data modeling, DAX measures, interactive visuals
- SQL — Data extraction and transformation  
- Excel — Data cleaning and preparation

## Dashboard Pages

### Page 1: Executive Overview
- Net Attrition: 237 employees
- Total Employees: 1,470 | Active: 1,233
- Attrition Rate: 16.1%
- Overtime Rate: 28.3%
- High Risk Employees: 77
- Average Tenure: 7 years

### Page 2: Attrition Deep Dive
- Highest attrition: Under 30 age group (28%)
- 54% of employees who left were working overtime
- Sales Representative: highest attrition role at 40%
- Attrition peaks in the first 2 years at company

### Page 3: Compensation & Satisfaction
- Employees who left earned $2,046/month less than those who stayed
- Avg income (left): $4,787 | Avg income (stayed): $6,833
- Top 20 high-risk employees identified by risk score
- Research & Development has highest job satisfaction (Level 4: 30.7%)

## Key Insights
1. Sales Representatives have 40% attrition — highest of any role
2. Overtime is a major driver — 54% of leavers worked overtime
3. Low income + low satisfaction + overtime = highest churn risk
4. Early tenure (0–2 years) is the most critical retention window

## Dataset
IBM HR Employee Attrition Dataset — available on Kaggle
1,470 rows | 35 columns
